# Presentation Notes

## One-minute pitch

I am developing an Astrophysical Structure Field model for dwarf-galaxy halo
diversity. The idea is not to replace gravity, but to test whether unresolved
baryonic organization can be represented by a dimensionless field whose
gradients create measurable effective-potential corrections in simulations.
The first test holds mass density fixed and changes only the structure field,
then compares potentials, accelerations, test-particle orbits, and rotation
curve diagnostics.

## Best single sentence

ASF is a phenomenological subgrid field that tests whether unresolved baryonic
organization, not just mass density, can influence effective potentials in
dwarf-galaxy simulations.

## Likely objections

- "Is this new gravity?" No. It is an effective computational closure.
- "Is this arbitrary?" V1 is phenomenological; the research task is to
  constrain or reject it.
- "Is this double-counting?" It can be if applied to resolved physics; it must
  represent unresolved structure only.
- "What would falsify it?" Instability, unphysical parameters, poor diagnostics,
  or no improvement over mass-only controls.
